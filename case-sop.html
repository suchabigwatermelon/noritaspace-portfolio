Case note · Project 01

科创训练营现场执行 SOP

Turning scattered on-site tasks into a clear execution workflow for education program operations.

  • Education Operations
  • SOP
  • Event Execution
  • AI-assisted Workflow

01

Project Overview

This case documents how I structured the on-site execution tasks for a technology commercialization bootcamp into a clear SOP for team coordination.

02

Context

A technology commercialization bootcamp involves many on-site details, including participant check-in, classroom preparation, printed materials, name cards, lecturer reception, PPT testing, classroom order, break reminders, meal guidance, certificate support, and exam-related coordination.

These tasks may look small individually, but without a clear workflow, they can easily lead to missed steps, repeated communication, and unnecessary pressure during live execution.

03

Challenge

The challenge was not a lack of tasks, but a lack of structure.

The on-site team needed to know what to do before class, during class, during breaks, after class, and on special sessions such as the first morning and the final certificate/exam session. A full SOP was necessary, but the group-message version also needed to be short enough for people to actually read and follow.

04

My Role

I organized the scattered operational tasks, grouped them by time and scenario, clarified which tasks were recurring and which only applied to specific sessions, and converted them into a structured SOP.

I also separated the complete workflow from the simplified group-message version, so the team could use different levels of detail in different communication contexts.

05

Process

Four steps from scattered responsibilities to a practical team workflow.

01

Collected scattered tasks

Listed all on-site responsibilities from check-in and materials to lecturer support and classroom coordination.

02

Grouped by time and scenario

Separated tasks by first morning, regular sessions, breaks, after-class transitions, and final certificate/exam support.

03

Structured the SOP

Converted the task list into a clear workflow that could be reused by the on-site team.

04

Created a simplified message

Condensed the SOP into a shorter group-message version for quick team communication.

06

Output

  • A structured SOP for on-site execution
  • A simplified group-message version for team coordination
  • A clearer checklist-style workflow for recurring bootcamp operations

07

Evidence / Sample Output

A simplified and anonymized sample structure of the SOP output.

Sample output

Sample SOP structure

Before class

  • Prepare materials, name cards, water, PPT, and classroom equipment.
  • Confirm lecturer arrival and classroom readiness.

During class

  • Maintain classroom order.
  • Respond to lecturer needs.
  • Prepare for break transitions.

After class

  • Escort lecturer.
  • Replace used water.
  • Prepare materials for the next session.

Special sessions

  • First morning: confidentiality forms, handouts, badges, seating cards.
  • Final afternoon: certificate support and exam coordination.
